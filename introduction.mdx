## Welcome to BlankDrive

BlankDrive is a **client-side, zero-knowledge vault** that puts you in complete control of your sensitive data. Store passwords, notes, TOTP secrets, and encrypted files, all synced securely to your own Google Drive account.

<Note>
  **Zero-Knowledge Architecture**: Your data is encrypted on your device before it ever reaches the cloud. BlankDrive never sees your plaintext data or master password.
</Note>

## Why BlankDrive?

BlankDrive is designed for people who want cloud-backed storage without sending plaintext to any third-party backend.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Client-Side Only" icon="laptop">

## Key Features

### Secure Storage
- **Password Manager**: Store unlimited passwords with username, URL, and notes
- **Secure Notes**: Encrypted notes for sensitive information
- **TOTP Support**: Built-in 2FA token generator
- **File Encryption**: Upload and encrypt files up to any size (chunked at 20 MB)

### Cloud Sync
- **Google Drive Integration**: Automatic sync with your Google account
- **Storage Modes**: Choose between visible (`public`) or hidden (`appDataFolder`) storage
- **Restore Anywhere**: Pull your encrypted vault from any device with `blankdrive init --restore`

### Security Features
- **Zero-Knowledge**: Your master password never leaves your device
- **Duress Mode**: Special unlock mode that shows decoy entries
- **Security Audit**: Password strength analyzer and breach checker
- **Auto-Lock**: Configurable timeout to lock vault when idle

### Multiple Interfaces
- **CLI**: Fast command-line interface (`blankdrive` or `BLANK`)
- **Interactive Shell**: Rich interactive mode with additional commands
- **Web UI**: Local web console at `localhost:4310`
- **Desktop App**: Native Tauri-based desktop application

## Typical Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Local Vault">
    You create a local vault protected by your master password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Entries">
    You add passwords, notes, and files locally.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Encrypt & Sync">
    BlankDrive encrypts data on-device, then uploads encrypted blobs to your Google Drive.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Restore Anywhere">
    On a new machine, restore using the same Google account and master password.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Real-World Use Cases

- **Secure personal document vault**: IDs, contracts, medical records, financial statements
- **Encrypted backup**: Off-device backup of sensitive notes and credentials
- **Cross-device access**: Access your vault from any machine via your Google Drive
- **Team password sharing**: Share encrypted vault entries (with careful key management)

## Security Specifications

<Info>
  BlankDrive uses industry-standard cryptography:
  - **Encryption**: AES-256-GCM with 12-byte IV and 16-byte auth tag
  - **Key Derivation**: Argon2id with time cost 3, memory cost 64 MB, parallelism 4
  - **Sub-key Derivation**: HKDF-SHA256 with unique contexts (`index`, `entry`, `metadata`)
  - **AAD Binding**: Each entry is bound to its ID for integrity verification
</Info>

## Trust Boundary

**What BlankDrive Does:**
- ✅ Runs entirely client-side (no project backend)
- ✅ Encrypts all data before cloud upload
- ✅ Uses direct OAuth with Google (local loopback + PKCE)

**What BlankDrive Does Not Guarantee:**
- ⚠️ If your machine is compromised while vault is unlocked, secrets can be exposed
- ⚠️ Losing your master password means encrypted data cannot be recovered
- ⚠️ Hidden mode improves privacy but is not a substitute for endpoint security

<Warning>
  **IMPORTANT**: Your master password is the ONLY way to decrypt your vault. If you lose it, your data is unrecoverable. Store it safely!
</Warning>
